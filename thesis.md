Modern Intelligence Technology Company (MITC) is a Bloomington, IN based startup providing hardware and software solutions for surveillance, intelligence, and information technology in support of public safety operations for small communities, specializing in local, on-premise, and self-hosted data storage and processing.

The City of Bloomington, IN is soliciting bids for an automated license plate reader (ALPR) camera network to replace their now disgraced relationship with Flock (as well as potential other products/services). **Flock is treated throughout MITC’s package as the competitive reference** for vendor-national, cloud-centric ALPR; MITC instead specifies **local custody** and **COTS ONVIF cameras** with **minimal on-pole complexity** (see technical specification).

Critically, in addition to the physical camera installations themselves, the data must be transferred, processed, and held locally, on a server located in-city.

Community maps (e.g. [DeFlock — Bloomington area](https://maps.deflock.org/?lat=39.1670&lng=-86.5343&zoom=11.00)) suggest **on the order of ~40** prior **Flock** endpoints—**not** an official census—so **budget and capacity** should be reconciled against **City purchasing records** and field surveys during procurement.

MITC intends to submit a bid for this contract; we intend to operate solely open-source software and protocols, and, to the greatest extent possible, open-source, or at least commodity/COTS, camera and network infrastructure hardware.

Generate a complete, lean, business plan; hardware and software specification for the ALPR proposal; comprehensive proposal paper; pitch deck; and contract bid cover/intro letter.
