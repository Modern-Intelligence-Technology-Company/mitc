Dear Clerk of the Council, Purchasing Agent, and designated evaluation team,

Modern Intelligence Technology Company (**MITC**), a **Bloomington, Indiana** company, is pleased to submit this package in response to the City’s anticipated procurement for **ALPR** capabilities following the City’s decision to **transition** its prior vendor relationship while strengthening **privacy protections, transparency, accountability, and public trust** (City news release, April 15, 2026).

MITC specializes in **local intelligence infrastructure** for small and mid-sized communities: **self-hosted** data processing, **open protocols**, and **commodity/COTS** camera and sensor hardware—so municipal partners retain **operational and legal control** over sensitive information.

### What we are proposing

MITC offers an **end-to-end program** to design, deploy, and operate a **Bloomington-located** ALPR platform in which **capture, recognition, indexing, operator access, audit logging, retention, and evidentiary export** occur on **servers physically situated in Bloomington**, on networks governed by the City. **Field sites stay deliberately simple—COTS imagers (see technical specification for manufacturer links), minimal pole complexity, and no mast-mounted “smart” ALPR**—so physical damage or theft does not compromise City-controlled inference and data. Our technical approach is **open-source-first** (Linux container platform, PostgreSQL metadata store, S3-compatible object storage such as **MinIO**, Prometheus/Grafana observability, and auditable open ALPR inference components), with **ONVIF/RTSP** camera interoperability to avoid proprietary lock-in.

We have aligned narrative scope to the **City’s public footprint summary** (11 fixed ALPR, 4 fixed video, 4 mobile trailers) **and** planned central **capacity and budget envelopes** against a **higher-density inventory** (~**40** **Flock**-class positions visible on the community **[DeFlock map](https://maps.deflock.org/?lat=39.1670&lng=-86.5343&zoom=11.00)**—**not** a substitute for an official as-built, but a **fiscal guardrail** until purchasing and field surveys reconcile counts. MITC stands ready to **bind** quantities to the formal solicitation **site list**.

### Why this matters for Bloomington

Bloomington’s leadership has made clear that if ALPR is used, it must be used under **narrow parameters** and **clear public safeguards**. An architecture that **defaults to local custody** and **transparent software supply chains** is structurally easier for the **Common Council**, residents, and oversight partners to **audit** than systems that centralize operational data in vendor clouds or national networks. MITC’s proposal is intentionally designed to **operationalize** the City’s stated governance commitments—not merely reference them in marketing language.
